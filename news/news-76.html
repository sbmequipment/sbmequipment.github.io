Malaysian bauxite exports will be depleted three years later

CRU, the metal industry analyst, said it expects the export volume of bauxite to 12 million tons this year, but the export volume will be depleted three years later, according to the CRU, a metals industry analyst firm. China's bauxite supply shortages were delayed, in part because Malaysia has increased exports of bauxite to China. It is expected that by 2020, the market will need an additional 28 million bauxite supply, and by 2030 the market will reach 89 million tons, to 2040, you need 170 million tons.

Malaysia extended the ban on bauxite mining until the end of the year

Reuters reported on Sept. 7 that the Malaysian Minister of the Environment said at a news conference on Wednesday that it had decided to extend the moratorium on bauxite mining for more than three months to December 31. The deadline for the previous ban is September 14.

Over the past two years, Malaysia has been largely unregulated in the development of bauxite mining industry, but the crazy exploitation of the public strongly dissatisfied, many people complain about water pollution and environmental damage.
